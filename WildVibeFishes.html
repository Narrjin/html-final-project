Habitats and Lifestyle

Dolphins, captivating marine mammals, inhabit a diverse range of aquatic environments. These intelligent creatures are found in oceans worldwide, as well as in some rivers. While most dolphins prefer coastal areas, some venture into shallow waters farther from the coast. Their habitats vary based on environmental factors and food availability. Dolphins are well-adapted for life in water, with streamlined bodies, flippers, and a layer of blubber for insulation. Their curved beaks give them a perpetual smile, and their tails move vertically as they swim. Despite lacking a strong sense of smell, dolphins excel in communication, social behavior, and acrobatics. Their conservation status varies across species, with bottlenose dolphins considered of Least Concern, while approximately 10 species face severe threats
